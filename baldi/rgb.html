quick prelude

Okay so basically some baldi lore people noticed that "pure" shades of the colors Red Green and Blue appear together in a lot of contexts. i originally didn't think any of this until Chex (cheatexp) came up with a theory that actually gives the color pairing meaning instead of just pointing at it and saying it exists. their theory is here if you wish to read it, but the gist of its interpretation of RGB is that they both correspond to the characters NULL, Baldi, Badsum, and to verticality based on the placeholder tiles (Down, "Walls" and Up), and that it means all three of the characters need to work together to uncorrupt the game. There's also evidence of 99 being associated with yellow, as a separate but adjacent thing to RGB.

I really like this idea , but also i think it'd be useful to compile all instances of RGB so that it's easier for people to draw their own conclusions about RGB in the context of Baldi's Basics. so here's that

BTW there's a "brainrot meter" inspired by Andrew Cunningham's Gaster motif video. the further to the left the brainrot meter is, the more likely i think it is to be intentional (not specifically to chex's interpretation, just in general,) although there's doubt with all of these since there hasn't been any direct confirmation that RGB means anything unlike 99. I just think this meter is useful to have as I will be including *every instance* of these colors together.

Baldi's Books

Origin: Baldi's Basics Classic Remastered (2022)

In Baldi's Basement, there's a bunch of books that all have the same text inside but have different covers.
The first three use RGB, with 1 being red, 2 being green, and 3 being blue.
The next three use CMY (the inverse of RGB), but it's not in the right order there.
I personally don't think much of these being RGB, though I do think it's weird how he says "Don't pay attention to anything, that's how the pros win!" in the books. but that's unrelated to it being RGB.... i think
The association of 1 as red, 2 as green, and 3 as blue is the biggest takeaway for me, since this association is later reused in the map markers and Laboratory posters.

Banana

Origin: Baldi's Basics Classic v1.3 (2018)

i'm breaking chronological order because starting with the banana would make me sound more stupid than starting with baldi's design did
